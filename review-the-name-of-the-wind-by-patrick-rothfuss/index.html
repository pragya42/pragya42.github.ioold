Review: The name of the wind by Patrick Rothfuss 

Is this the best book ever written, with characters worth writing thousands of opinion peices for? Or is it just another insufferable fantasy fluff? "Well here's another opinion peice".  So while reading about this book I came across two types of reviews, well that's what intriguied me a lot about it. While reading I found myself siding with one and then another. I couldn't make a stand. So well here it is, my version of the Non alignment movement.  A spoiler warning is in order.

Intro- The name of the Wind is the first book in the series of the Kingkiller chronicles. I have to say I love the name the kingkiller chronicles. 

For the entire book I was thiking when will this King show up :p (I think he already has! IFKWIM)

magic system

writing style - The writing style is extremel detailed, simple yet intricate. If you look at Patrick rothfuss' and the read the book you can totally. The first person narrative style even the POV characters and that's where my issue is, He has done it very well, it is like this exceptionally bright kid would see the world. It is like  and then remeber it nostraligiclly later when he's kind of relating. And I do think it has been done purposefully, coz there's this whole exchange between bast and Kvothe about Dinnah not being that perfect. Which also gave me si=ome theories about how he is related to Kvothe. I haven't read the 2d book but I've watched way too many videos.

But there are some serious pacing issues, which I've heard gets worse later. Everytime that woman stepped on screen , I just went off to sleep. Not hating on the chracter as a person but just this potrayal of this male gazey view of her IDK. But if that's the case what if ambrose was'nt too terible as well? 

characters, insufferable pacing issues, whta is happening

love the background and the lore but eww

The idea etc. sound lovely but who wants to see a great world through the eyes of a 13 year old perfect self obseesed kid. Like suppose hermiune narrates harry potter.

The

 

The writing style, lore and magic system is very very good, but is it worth bearing insufferable characters and pacing issues? I don't know! I'll wait until the third book (Doors of stone)comes out. Untill then I'll watch every video, read every blog about the worldbuilding. Plus it has the best author introductions ever.

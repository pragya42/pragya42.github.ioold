Quite often termed as the “Lolita” of the me too era, My dark Vanessa by Kate Elizabeth Russel is an incredibly powerful, gut wrenching and well balanced book on a very heavy topic. Vanessa Wye is a victim of sexual abuse and grooming, and she’s still coming to terms with it. The story goes back and forth in time where we see a fifteen year old Vanessa in a relationship with her teacher.We then see her navigating college and adult life. I have no cons for this book, and to me it’s a must read. It tackles a subject this difficult with utmost sincerity, care, and realism. Every character in this book is humane, including the predator. The author fleshes him and his doings really well. It’s horrifying to see that someone as normal as him can turn out to be so horrible. According to some people the last 150 pages of the book are a bit slow paced and could’ve been omitted. But I completely differ, the last pages show Vanessa in a broader perspective. They show other aspects of the problem. The tone of the book is narrator appropriate and perfect. And I absolutely love the way it refers to “Loilita” and Nabakov in general. Having said that , the author has been brutally honest and there are a few scenes which might be difficult and disturbing for some people ( they were for me, but I won’t count that as a con) To conclude I’ll leave you with a question “Can you rape the willing ?” P.S. I’ve tried to be as subtle and spoiler free as possible , and my apologies, if things have still passed by.
